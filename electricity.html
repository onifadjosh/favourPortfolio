Electricity

The Electricity Payment Website was designed to provide users with a seamless and reliable way to purchase electricity tokens, pay bills, and track usage all in one place. The goal was to create a fast, user-friendly interface that eliminates the common frustrations people face with traditional payment platforms from failed transactions to confusing navigation. With a modern layout, clear CTAs, and mobile responsiveness, the platform ensures that users can complete their payments quickly and with confidence.

Problem Statement

Despite the growing demand for online electricity bill payments in Nigeria, most existing platforms are far from seamless. Users often encounter clunky interfaces, unclear payment flows, delayed token delivery, and frequent transaction failures. These issues not only disrupt the payment process but also lead to frustration, distrust, and poor user retention. The lack of intuitive design, real-time feedback, and efficient user support on many of these platforms creates a gap for a smoother, more reliable, and user-centered experience.

Solution

To address the challenges users face with existing electricity payment websites, I designed a streamlined web experience focused on clarity, speed, and trust. The platform features a clean and intuitive interface that guides users through the entire payment processâ€”from meter selection to token confirmation with minimal friction. Real-time feedback, simplified forms, and responsive design ensure that users can pay their bills or recharge tokens effortlessly, whether on desktop or mobile. Visual hierarchy and clear CTAs enhance usability, while secure payment integration builds confidence with every transaction.

Overview

The Electricity Payment Website was created to simplify how users purchase electricity tokens and manage their payments online. Designed with a user-first approach, the platform addresses common pain points such as unclear interfaces, failed transactions, and limited support. The goal was to build a digital product that combines visual clarity, responsive design, and functional efficiency making it easy for users to recharge prepaid meters, view billing history, and complete secure payments in just a few clicks. This project showcases a complete end-to-end experience from research and wireframing to high-fidelity design and prototyping.

Role

UI/UX Designer

UX Process

  1. Understanding the Problem: To begin, I reviewed existing electricity payment websites and identified key issues users frequently encounter: poor UI structure, failed transactions, lack of real-time feedback, and little to no trust signals. I also spoke informally with a few everyday users of prepaid meters to better understand their expectations when trying to recharge online
  2. Defining User Goals & Flows: I mapped out the most critical tasks users needed to complete: buying tokens, selecting meter type, confirming payment, and receiving receipts or token codes. I then created user flows that simplified these actions into as few steps as possible to avoid confusion or drop-offs.
  3. Wireframing the Experience: Based on the defined flows, I sketched low-fidelity wireframes to explore layout ideas and information hierarchy. The focus was to keep actions clear and reduce cognitive load by using progressive disclosure and clean spacing.
  4. Designing the Interface: Using Figma, I developed a clean, responsive UI that aligned with modern visual standards. I prioritized legibility, large clickable areas, mobile responsiveness, and a consistent visual hierarchy. Design elements like status indicators, success states, and visual feedback were added to reassure users throughout their journey.
  5. Prototyping & Testing: I created an interactive prototype to simulate the user journey from start to finish. I then shared it with 3â€“5 users for informal usability testing. Feedback highlighted areas that needed improvement, such as improving form field spacing and making error messages more visible.
  6. Refinement & Finalization: Based on the insights, I refined the designs, added micro-interactions, and ensured accessibility elements like contrast and keyboard navigation were considered. The final handoff included design documentation and specifications ready for development.
